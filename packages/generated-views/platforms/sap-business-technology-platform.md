1. Open SAP BTP Getting Started. Success signal: Getting Started Workflows is visible. Sources: S1.
2. Choose the global-account model. Success signal: The selected model matches intended use, ownership, lifecycle, and commercial requirements. Sources: S2, S4.
3. Create or sign in to an SAP account and obtain access to the selected BTP global account. Success signal: The SAP BTP cockpit opens at the global account. Sources: S2, S3.
4. Choose Cloud Foundry, ABAP, Kyma, or another service-specific workflow. Success signal: The selected guide matches the workload and is available in the account and region. Sources: S1, S4, S6.
5. Choose the concrete first workload and follow its prerequisites, provisioning, tools, deployment, and verification. Success signal: A guide-specific resource or workload result can be observed. Sources: S5, S6, S7.
6. Open sap.com and select Log On. Success signal: The user can either sign in or choose registration. Sources: S3.
7. If no SAP account exists, select Register, complete the required fields, and submit the form. Success signal: SAP reports that an activation email was sent. Sources: S3.
8. Deliver the SAP account activation email. Success signal: The activation email arrives in the inbox. Sources: S3.
9. Open the activation link and complete account activation. Success signal: A success message confirms that the account is activated. Sources: S3.
10. Open SAP BTP Trial, select Try Now, and follow the sign-in instructions. Success signal: The BTP trial region and account-creation controls are visible. Sources: S3, S4.
11. Choose the trial geographic region and select Create Account. Success signal: The setup progress dialog begins. Sources: S3, S4.
12. Provision the SAP BTP trial account and its initial resources. Success signal: The setup dialog reports completion and enables Continue. Sources: S3.
13. Select Continue to open the SAP BTP cockpit. Success signal: The cockpit loads and shows the trial account. Sources: S3.
14. Open the trial subaccount tile and inspect its status, Cloud Foundry API endpoint, and space. Success signal: The subaccount view exposes its state plus the Cloud Foundry API and space information. Sources: S3.
15. Open Services, then Service Marketplace, and verify that the service catalog and filter control are visible. Success signal: Service tiles and the marketplace filter bar are visible. Sources: S3.
