**Developer goal:** Create a Sumo Logic trial account and reach the preloaded Kickstart sample dashboard that shows key performance metrics without configuring ingestion.

**Meaningful success:** A newly activated trial user sees preloaded Kickstart sample data and an application, business, or security dashboard without configuring a collector or ingesting real data.

**Evidence boundary:** demonstrated-terminal-state. The quickstart presents the sample dashboard before its onboarding checklist and before Step 1 for the developer's own data. It explicitly says no setup, data ingestion, or firewall configuration is required, so later searches, alerts, app installation, team invitations, and real-data collection are after the earliest visible value state.

## Documented path

1. Open the official Get Started home and follow Sign Up and Activate Your Account, then Quickstart. Success signal: The page links signup and activation immediately before the quickstart. Sources: S1.
2. Open the Sumo Logic Free Trial page and select Continue with email. Success signal: Business email and contact fields are visible. Sources: S2, S4.
3. Enter business email, first name, last name, company, and phone, accept the Service License Agreement, choose whether to receive communications, and select Start free trial. Success signal: Please check your inbox and the submitted email address are displayed. Sources: S2, S4.
4. Open the Sumo Logic activation email and select Activate Now. Success signal: Region, organization, country, and password controls are visible. Sources: S2, S4.
5. Select the AWS region for data storage, confirm first name, last name, organization, and country, supply state or province when required, and create a password. Success signal: All required controls pass their displayed validation requirements. Sources: S2, S4.
6. Accept the required Service License Agreement and any applicable regional personal-information choices, then select the account-completion control. Success signal: The official signup guide states that activated accounts are guided through personalized onboarding. Sources: S2, S4.
7. If the activation flow does not preserve an authenticated session, sign in with the registered email address and password. Success signal: The developer reaches the authenticated Get Started experience. Sources: S2, S3.
8. Display the Get Started page with preloaded Kickstart sample data and sample dashboards on first login. Success signal: Preloaded sample metrics and log-derived dashboard content are visible. Sources: S2, S3.

## Documented friction gates

- Google SSO, business email, and AWS Marketplace change identity, verification, agreement, and billing actions. Stage 2. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S2, S4.
- The business-email path requires work contact and organization data across an initial and a completion form. Stage 2. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S4.
- Email activation must be completed within three days or signup must be repeated. Stage 2. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S2.
- The developer must select the data-storage region based on residency requirements. Stage 2. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S4.
- The direct signup path requires acceptance of the Service License Agreement and can show region-specific personal-information choices. Stage 2. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S4.
