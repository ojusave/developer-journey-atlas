## Documented path

1. Open the Databricks Get started tutorials and select Query and visualize data. Success signal: The tutorial requirements and Step 1 are visible. Sources: S1, S4.
2. Sign up for Databricks Free Edition or obtain access to an eligible workspace. Success signal: The new workspace opens and serverless compute is available. Sources: S2, S3.
3. Click New in the workspace sidebar, then click Notebook. Success signal: A blank notebook opens in the workspace. Sources: S4.
4. Paste one vendor-provided query for samples.nyctaxi.trips into the empty cell. Success signal: The cell contains a syntactically complete query. Sources: S4.
5. Press Shift+Enter to run the cell. Success signal: Query results appear in the notebook and focus moves to the next cell. Sources: S4.
6. Click the plus control next to Table and select Visualization. Success signal: The visualization editor opens. Sources: S4.
7. Configure the chart as Bar with fare_amount on X and trip_distance on Y. Success signal: The editor preview reflects the selected axes. Sources: S4.
8. Set aggregation to Average and Group by to pickup_zip. Success signal: The preview matches the tutorial image and grouping. Sources: S4.
9. Click Save in the visualization editor. Success signal: The chart is retained as a visualization tab in the notebook output, completing the tutorial. Sources: S4.

## Documented friction gates

- Workspace access is required, and the choice between Free Edition and free trial depends on intended use. Stage 2. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S2, S3.
- Free Edition requires no payment, while the business free trial can require a credit card for paid continuation. Stage 2. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S2.
- The workspace must have Unity Catalog and the developer must be allowed to use or create compute. Stage 5. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S4.
- Free Edition compute is subject to daily fair-usage quotas and can be unavailable after the quota is exceeded. Stage 5. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S3.
