## Documented friction gates

- Public and private app intent changes the required workspace and later authentication model. Stage 2. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S1, S2.
- Developer signup requests work email, full name, password, and company name, or Google authentication. Stage 3. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S3.
- Submitting developer signup agrees to Intercom's Terms of Service, Developer Terms, and Privacy Policy. Stage 3. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S3.
- Development workspaces are US-only, and private sign-in requires selecting United States, Europe, or Australia before authentication. Stage 3. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S1, S4.
- Development workspaces are limited to 20 users or leads and omit production functionality including outbound email and push notifications. Stage 3. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S1.
- The access token grants access to private Intercom data and must not be shared with third parties. Stage 3. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S2.
