## Documented friction gates

- An Okta Integrator Free Plan org and administrator access are required; signup needs a unique eligible business email and a location accepted by Okta's security controls. Stage 2. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S5, S6.
- Signup requires acceptance of the Integrator Free Plan Agreement and Privacy Policy by someone authorized to bind the represented entity. Stage 4. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S6, S7.
- The developer must receive the verification email and use its activation link before setting the password and authenticators. Stage 5. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S5.
- The free org is limited to 10 active users and deactivates after 180 consecutive days without a user sign-in unless an app is submitted to the OIN. Stage 7. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S5.
- The administrator needs sufficient role permissions to create and configure app integrations. Stage 7. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S3, S4.
- Existing orgs without API Access Management may require support enablement or a Console-only alternative. Stage 7. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S3.
- The developer must choose sign-in versus API protection and an application architecture. Stage 8. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S1, S2.
- A language or framework choice is required before atomic implementation steps exist. Stage 9. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S1.
