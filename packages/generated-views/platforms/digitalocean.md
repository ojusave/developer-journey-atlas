## Documented friction gates

- The documented app flow requires the authenticated Control Panel, but current public App Platform docs do not enumerate the signup screens. Stage 3. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S2, S3, S6.
- Signup or login can trigger verification or account lock handling. The official support surface directs a locked signup to support and discusses emailed verification codes, but does not locate those states in the App Platform path. Stage 3. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S6.
- DigitalOcean may require a payment method to verify identity. The exact eligibility and position of this gate are not stated by the App Platform quickstart. Stage 3. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S7.
- The sample flow branches between creating a GitHub repository and deploying directly from the template repository. Stage 6. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S3.
- Detected resource settings depend on the starter and resource type, and a web service must bind its HTTP port to 0.0.0.0. Stage 8. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S3, S4.
- The developer must review pricing. Only qualifying static sites have a free tier; other service resources are billed by selected size and usage. Stage 12. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S5.
- App Platform builds and deploys before the public URL can be verified. The quickstart does not document a typical duration. Stage 14. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S2, S4, S8.
