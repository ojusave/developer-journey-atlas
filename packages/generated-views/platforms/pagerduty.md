## Documented path

1. Open Trial Account Onboarding and follow its signup link. Success signal: The Work Email field is visible. Sources: S1.
2. Enter an eligible Work Email and click Get Started. Success signal: The First Name, Last Name, Company, and Country fields appear. Sources: S1.
3. Enter First Name, Last Name, Company, and Country, then click Next Step. Success signal: The password field appears. Sources: S1.
4. Enter a password and click Next Step. Success signal: The personalized subdomain field appears. Sources: S1.
5. Enter a personalized PagerDuty subdomain that is at least five characters long and not already in use. Success signal: The form accepts the subdomain. Sources: S1.
6. Select USA or EU as the service region. Success signal: One region is selected. Sources: S1.
7. Review the Terms of Service and Privacy Policy, check the acknowledgment and agreement box if accepted, then click Create Account. Success signal: The onboarding flow opens at Step 1: Create a Service. Sources: S1.
8. Enter a descriptive Service Name for a service the team owns and click Continue. Success signal: Onboarding advances to Configure Inbound Integrations. Sources: S1.
9. At Configure Inbound Integrations, click Skip step for the neutral first path. Success signal: Onboarding advances to Configure Notification Methods. Sources: S1.
10. Check Phone & SMS and enter a phone number; check Email and enter an email address. Success signal: Phone, SMS, and email destinations are shown in the notification test form. Sources: S1.
11. Click Send Test Notification. Success signal: PagerDuty asks the developer to confirm the details. Sources: S1.
12. Confirm the details are correct and click Send Test Notification again. Success signal: PagerDuty reports that the tests were sent. Sources: S1.
13. Receive and verify the test notifications through the configured methods. Success signal: The developer receives the test email, voice call, and SMS. Sources: S1.
14. Download the PagerDuty mobile app from the listed Apple App Store or Google Play link. Success signal: The app is present on the device. Sources: S1.
15. Click Next after the mobile-app step. Success signal: The schedule invitation form appears. Sources: S1.
16. Click Skip step at Configure an On-Call Schedule for the single-developer neutral route. Success signal: Onboarding advances to Connect With Your Chat Tools. Sources: S1.
17. At Connect With Your Chat Tools, choose to add extensions later and complete the final step without adding one. Success signal: PagerDuty directs the developer to the account. Sources: S1.
18. Confirm the final redirect opens the new PagerDuty account and the first service is available. Success signal: The account UI loads under the selected subdomain and region with the created service, after notification tests have succeeded. Sources: S1.

## Documented friction gates

- PagerDuty trial signup rejects common consumer email domains and requires a work identity plus company and country data. Stage 2. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S1.
- The developer must secure a unique personalized subdomain at least five characters long. Stage 5. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S1.
- USA or EU data-hosting region must be selected before account creation. Stage 6. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S1.
- Terms of Service and Privacy Policy must be reviewed and affirmatively accepted to create the account. Stage 7. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S1.
- Inbound integration selection is optional but tool-specific; selecting one moves real event delivery into a separate guide. Stage 9. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S1.
- The guide recommends configuring and testing email, voice, and SMS, requiring reachable external channels and successful delivery. Stage 10. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S1.
- The onboarding sequence includes downloading the mobile app from one of two external stores before clicking Next. Stage 14. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S1.
- Creating a schedule now dispatches teammate invitations and creates a Default escalation policy; skipping defers both. Stage 16. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S1.
- Adding a chat extension requires an external authentication workflow; the neutral path defers it. Stage 17. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S1.
