2. Open Create your sandbox account, provide a valid email address, and agree to the Dwolla Developer Terms and Service. Success signal: Dwolla sends an email asking the developer to verify the address. Sources: S1, S2, S3.
3. Open the Dwolla email and verify the email address. Success signal: The developer is redirected to the Sandbox Dashboard. Sources: S1, S2.
4. Create the sandbox account, application, Superhero Savings Bank funding source, and test balance. Success signal: The Sandbox Dashboard is available. Sources: S1, S2.
5. In the Sandbox Dashboard, open Applications and locate the `client_id` and `client_secret`. Success signal: Both credential values are available. Sources: S1.
6. Open the Dwolla Quickstart. Success signal: The guide is open. Sources: S1.
7. Provide a valid email address and agree to the Developer Terms and Service in sandbox signup. Success signal: The verification email is sent. Sources: S1, S2, S3.
8. Verify the email address from the received message. Success signal: The browser redirects to the Sandbox Dashboard. Sources: S1, S2.
9. Provision the sandbox account, application, test funding source, and $5,000 balance. Success signal: The automatically created application is available. Sources: S1, S2.
10. Open Applications and retrieve the `client_id` and `client_secret`. Success signal: Both values are visible. Sources: S1.
11. Base64-encode `client_id:client_secret` for HTTP Basic authentication. Success signal: The encoded credential is available for the request header. Sources: S1.
12. POST to `https://api-sandbox.dwolla.com/token` with Basic authorization, form content type, and `grant_type=client_credentials`. Success signal: The JSON response contains `access_token`, `token_type`, and `expires_in`. Sources: S1.
13. GET `https://api-sandbox.dwolla.com/` with the HAL+JSON Accept header and bearer access token. Success signal: The request returns a JSON Root resource. Sources: S1, S2.
14. Read the account and customers links from the Root response. Success signal: `_links.account.href` and `_links.customers.href` are present, reaching the guide's You did it terminal. Sources: S1.
