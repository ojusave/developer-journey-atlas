**Developer goal:** Select a Dynamics 365 application and, if Business Central is selected, deploy and run Microsoft's generated HelloWorld AL extension on an online sandbox.

**Meaningful success:** The generated HelloWorld extension compiles, publishes, and runs in the opened Business Central online sandbox session.

**Evidence boundary:** demonstrated-terminal-state. This is the documented terminal of the committed Business Central AL get-started candidate under workshop policy.

## Documented path

1. Open the Dynamics 365 developer resources matrix, identify the business application being extended, and select Business Central for this documented candidate. Success signal: The Business Central AL tutorial is selected for steps 2 through 9. Sources: S1, S2.
2. Use Microsoft's sandbox signup link to create or obtain a Dynamics 365 Business Central online sandbox tenant. Success signal: The Business Central online sandbox is provisioned and accessible. Sources: S3.
3. Download and install Visual Studio Code. Success signal: Visual Studio Code opens. Sources: S3.
4. Install the AL Language extension for Microsoft Dynamics 365 Business Central in Visual Studio Code. Success signal: AL commands, including AL: Go!, are available in Visual Studio Code. Sources: S3.
5. Open User Settings with Ctrl+Shift+P and make the documented telemetry choice before creating the project. Success signal: The developer has retained the default or saved telemetry.telemetryLevel as off. Sources: S3.
6. Run AL: Go!, choose a new empty folder and the Business Central version, then select Microsoft cloud sandbox as the server. Success signal: The generated project files appear in the selected folder. Sources: S3.
7. Enter the Business Central credentials used for the sandbox signup when Visual Studio Code requests them. Success signal: Visual Studio Code can connect and prepare the sandbox deployment. Sources: S3.
8. Press Ctrl+F5 to build, publish, and run the generated extension on the online sandbox without debugging. Success signal: The publish completes and Business Central opens the extension in the online sandbox. Sources: S3.
9. Verify that the generated HelloWorld sample has compiled and is running in the Business Central online sandbox. Success signal: The HelloWorld sample is visible and running in the opened sandbox session. Sources: S3.

## Documented friction gates

- Dynamics 365 is a suite, so product and developer job must be selected before one path exists. Stage 1. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S1, S2.
- Product trials, previews, licenses, and entitlements are separate and product-specific. Stage 2. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S2, S3.
- Development environment and toolchain differ among AL, Dataverse and Power Platform, and finance and operations applications. Stage 3. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S1, S3.
- Tenant and environment roles required to customize or deploy depend on the selected application. Stage 7. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S1, S2, S3.
- The AL extension must compile, package, upload, publish, and open in the online sandbox before HelloWorld can be verified. Stage 8. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S3.
- The Business Central candidate ends only when Microsoft's generated HelloWorld sample is compiled and running, not when the sandbox or project is merely created. Stage 9. Evidence state: documented requirement. Sources: S3.
